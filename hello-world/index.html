Research & Development // AI Implementation Toolkit The prerequisites

Hello,
World
(...of AI chaos)

Everyone skipped this part. They downloaded the AI tool, typed a prompt, got mediocre results and blamed the technology. The technology wasn't the problem. The missing prerequisites were. Three things have to exist before AI can do anything useful with your work: you need to know yourself well enough to give it context, you need to know what's safe to hand it and you need a process documented well enough that someone (or something) other than you can follow it. This is that checklist. In order. No skipping.

The Toolkit

Three
Steps

Each step produces something usable on its own. But they work in sequence because each one depends on what the step before it surfaces. Start Here builds the self-awareness. The Safety First Gate applies it to a specific project. The SOP Playbook Builder documents what survives the gate.

01
Context Onboarding
Start Here
AI without context produces generic output. This interactive tool builds your personal AI profile in ten minutes or fewer. Four cards. All multiple-choice. The output is a paste-ready markdown profile for ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini... whatever you're using. Card 1 alone takes two minutes and gives AI more useful information about you than most people ever provide.
Build Your Profile →
02
AI Project Readiness
The Safety First Gate
Six gates. 25 binary questions. All must pass. Before you hand AI a process to execute, this gate forces you to answer whether this specific project is safe, legal and ethical enough to proceed. Not a policy document. A decision tool you run once per project. GO, CONDITIONAL or NO-GO. If you can't pass the gate, the project isn't ready... and finding that out now costs nothing compared to finding out after.
Run the Gate →
03
Process Documentation
SOP Playbook Builder
"Just write down what you do" produces SOPs that are approximately half-complete while appearing finished. The missing half is the work your brain does automatically... the micro-checks, the pattern recognition, the split-second decisions you've made so many times they feel like instinct. This system runs you through four passes to surface what one pass structurally cannot. The result is instructions that actually transfer.
