ANONYMIZED REFERENCE TEMPLATES FOR CRISIS RESPONSE

A standardized approach to categorizing crises helps teams respond with speed and clarity. These templates address distinct incident types, offering structured guidance for rapid external communication. Verify facts and obtain legal review before distribution.

01 Generic Holding Statement

We're aware that [what happened] occurred at [location] [time]. We're looking into what happened and will share what we learn as soon as we can. In the meantime, [relevant immediate action or reassurance].

02 Natural Disaster

Our thoughts are with everyone affected by [disaster type] in [region/locations]. We're coordinating support for our employees and their families. If you're in an affected area, please follow local emergency guidance and reach out to your regional leader. We'll update you as we learn more.

03 Technology Outage

We experienced a technical issue with [service or product] starting [time] that affected [group]. We understand how much you rely on this, and we're sorry. Our team is working to restore service now. We'll provide updates via [channel] every [timeframe]. For urgent issues, contact [support].

04 Unexpected Death or Injury of an Employee

We are devastated by the loss of [employee name/role]. Our hearts go out to their family and loved ones. We're offering support to our team during this difficult time. Counseling and grief resources are available to anyone who needs them. We will honor their memory and [relevant commitment].

05 Personnel Crisis (Accusation)

We've learned of serious allegations involving [role title]. We take these matters deeply and are looking into what happened. We're committed to a workplace where everyone feels safe and respected. We'll work with [HR/legal] to respond appropriately. Our focus right now is on caring for those affected.

06 Personnel Crisis (Confirmed)

What [role title] did does not reflect who we are. We see the pain this has caused and we're sorry. [action taken]. We're committed to rebuilding trust through real change, not just words. If you've been affected and need support, we have resources available.

07 Merger or Acquisition

We're excited to share that we're joining forces with [organization]. This partnership strengthens our ability to [mission/benefit]. We know change creates questions. Your role and benefits [immediate clarification]. We'll share more details [timeframe] and are here to answer your questions.

08 Layoffs or Reduction in Force

We've made the difficult decision to reduce our workforce. We know this creates fear and uncertainty. We're being transparent about who is affected and providing [severance, benefits, support]. If your role is changing, you'll hear directly. We're committed to treating everyone with respect through this transition. [support resources] are available to all.

09 Product Delay, Damage, or Recall

We discovered that shipments of [product] were [contaminated/damaged/delayed] due to [reason]. We apologize for this. We're working around the clock to [replacement/refund/resolution] by [date]. Your trust matters to us, and we're taking this seriously.

10 Financial Crisis

We're facing significant financial challenges that require us to [restructure/seek capital/action]. We understand this news is concerning. Here's what it means for you: [impacts and support]. We remain committed to [core mission] and will keep you informed as we move forward.

11 Organizational Crisis

We're aware of [allegation/accusation type] involving [person/department]. These accusations are serious and we take them to heart. We're conducting a thorough investigation and have [immediate actions]. We recognize this shakes the trust we've built, and we're committed to making things right.

12 Regulatory or Compliance Issue

We recently learned we didn't meet [regulatory requirement/standard]. We're taking this seriously and have [immediate steps taken]. We're working with [agency/counsel] to address this, and we'll share updates [timeframe]. Thank you for your patience.

13 Lawsuit

We're aware of litigation that has been filed against us. We can't comment on the specific claims, but we remain committed to [core values/commitment that does not discuss the case]. We take our responsibilities seriously and will address this through the legal process.

14 Active Security Threat

We're aware of a security situation at [location]. Your safety comes first. Follow instructions from local authorities and emergency responders. Do not come to this facility unless you receive the all-clear. We'll keep you updated via [channel]. If you need support, resources are available.

15 Competitive Pressure Response

We're aware of market pressures from [competitor/market shift]. This reinforces why we exist: [core purpose]. We're focused on what we do best and where we can add value for you. We'll navigate this together and stay true to what matters.

Deployment Protocol

  • Verify bracketed fields against confirmed facts only. Cross-reference with incident command and primary sources before filling placeholders.
  • Obtain legal review for sensitive statements before external distribution. Route through designated legal counsel with copy to crisis communications lead.
  • Separate internal and external versions. Internal statements can include additional context. External statements must stay focused on key messages.
  • Assign a single primary spokesperson. One voice prevents confusion and mixed messaging.
  • Log messages with timestamp and distribution channel. Document approval chain, timing and template changes for post-crisis review.
  • Update stakeholders on a consistent schedule. Set a cadence (every 2 hours, every 4 hours) and maintain it regardless of new information.
  • Avoid speculation, timelines, or language that implies liability. Stick to verified facts and organizational values.
  • Maintain a media contact list for distribution. Test systems during normal periods to ensure rapid deployment when needed.
